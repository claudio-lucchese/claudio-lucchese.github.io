Elianto document annotation demo accepted at CIKM 2014!


Aug. 16 2014

Our demo paper Ceccarelli, D., Lucchese, C., Orlando, S., Perego, R., and Trani, S. “Manual Annotation of Semi-Structured Documents for Entity-Linking” was accepted at the 23rd ACM Intl. Conference on Information and Knowledge Management, Shanghai, China, 3–7 November 2014.

The Entity Linking (EL) problem consists in automatically linking short fragments of text within a document to entities in a given Knowledge Base like Wikipedia. Due to its impact in several text-understanding related tasks, EL is an hot research topic. The correlated problem of devising the most relevant entities mentioned in the document, a.k.a. salient entities (SE), is also attracting increasing interest. Unfortunately, publicly available evaluation datasets that contain accurate and supervised knowledge about mentioned entities and their relevance ranking are currently very poor both in number and quality. This lack makes very diﬃcult to compare diﬀerent EL and SE solutions on a fair basis, as well as to devise innovative techniques that relies on these datasets to train machine learning models, in turn used to automatically link and rank entities.

In this demo paper we propose a Web-deployed tool that allows to crowdsource the creation of these datasets, by supporting the collaborative human annotation of semi-structured documents. The tool, called ELIANTO, is actually an open source framework, which provides a user friendly and reactive Web interface to support both EL and SE labelling tasks, through a guided two-steps process.
